# GPT-4o vs DeepSeek-V3 vs GLM-4: Complete LLM Benchmark Comparison 2026

**Summary:** Three models, three ecosystems, one question: which should you use? GPT-4o (OpenAI) represents the US proprietary ceiling. DeepSeek-V3 is the cost-efficiency champion with MoE architecture. GLM-4 (Zhipu AI) is the open-weight contender with unique hybrid attention. We break down the numbers, the trade-offs, and which model wins for each use case.

## Comparison Overview

These three models represent fundamentally different philosophies in the 2026 LLM landscape. GPT-4o is the closed-source, API-only flagship from OpenAI — the safe default for most teams. DeepSeek-V3 uses a Mixture-of-Experts architecture (37B active out of 671B total parameters) to deliver strong performance at a fraction of the cost. GLM-4 (130B parameters) is fully open-weight, runs on your own hardware, and uses a hybrid bidirectional-autoregressive attention mechanism that gives it an edge on Chinese-language tasks.

## Analysis Per Use Case

### English-language tasks
GPT-4o is the strongest performer across MMLU (88.7%), GPQA Diamond (53.6%), and code generation (HumanEval 90.2%). It's the safe, high-quality default. DeepSeek-V3 trails by 2-3 points across most English benchmarks but costs 9x less — a trade-off that makes sense for high-volume, latency-tolerant workloads.

### Chinese-language tasks
GLM-4 leads on C-Eval (82.3% vs 80.1% for DeepSeek-V3, 78.5% for GPT-4o) and CMMLU (81.7%). Its hybrid attention was designed for Chinese linguistic structures, and it shows. If your primary workload is Chinese-language, GLM-4 is the best model — and you can self-host it.

### Math and STEM reasoning
DeepSeek-V3 dominates here. The MATH-500 gap (90.2% vs 76.6% for GPT-4o) is large enough to change your model selection if math is a core workload. The AIME 2024 result (39.2% vs 9.3%) makes this even clearer — DeepSeek-V3 is the best STEM reasoning model in this comparison.

### Code generation
GPT-4o is the strongest coder. HumanEval 90.2%, MBPP 87.6%. DeepSeek-V3 is close (88.4% and 85.1%) but the gap widens on harder benchmarks like LiveCodeBench. GLM-4 at 67.3% HumanEval is not competitive for coding tasks — it was not designed for this use case.

### Self-hosting and cost
GLM-4 is the only model with open weights — you can run it on your own hardware, fine-tune it, and deploy it air-gapped. DeepSeek-V3 is API-only but 9x cheaper than GPT-4o. If you need full control: GLM-4. If you need cheap API inference: DeepSeek-V3. If you need the best quality and don't mind paying: GPT-4o.

## Recommendations

### Use GPT-4o if:
- English-language quality is your top priority
- Code generation is a primary workload
- You have budget for the premium tier
- You need the safest, most reliable default

### Use DeepSeek-V3 if:
- Math and STEM reasoning are critical workloads
- You want 9x cost savings over GPT-4o with minimal quality trade-off
- You need strong bilingual English-Chinese capability
- You're running high-volume inference where cost matters

### Use GLM-4 if:
- You need self-hosted, air-gapped deployment
- Chinese-language tasks are your primary workload
- You need full model weight access for fine-tuning
- You have A100/H100-class hardware available
- Infrastructure cost is not your primary concern (free weights, not free compute)

## Bottom Line

There's no universal winner. GPT-4o for general quality, DeepSeek-V3 for math and cost-efficiency, GLM-4 for self-hosting and Chinese. The right choice depends on your workload, budget, and infrastructure. Teams that run all three — routing tasks to the best model per query — get the best of all worlds.
